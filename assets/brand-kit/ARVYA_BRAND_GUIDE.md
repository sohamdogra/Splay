# Arvya Brand Kit

Version 1.0 — future marketing standard  
Source priority: Figma design tokens → recurring Canva usage → this guide

## 1. Brand core

**Category**  
Source-backed AI deal execution for investment banking and private equity.

**Brand promise**  
Arvya turns fragmented deal activity into a persistent, source-cited deal memory inside the tools bankers already use.

**Primary audience**

- Investment-banking and private-equity deal teams
- Managing directors, deal leads, analysts, associates, and executive assistants
- Security, compliance, and technology stakeholders at financial institutions

**Brand attributes**  
Precise, credible, institutional, calm, useful, source-backed, approval-first.

**Positioning line**  
The source-backed AI deal execution layer for investment banking and private equity.

**Primary campaign line**  
Every deal deserves a memory.

**Secondary campaign line**  
Don’t ask AI. Ask the source.

**Proof-point hierarchy**

1. Source-cited answers from real deal materials
2. Outlook-native workflows
3. Approval-first actions
4. Persistent deal memory across team rotations
5. Enterprise-ready Microsoft 365 deployment

## 2. Logo system

The Arvya system has two assets:

- **Mark:** circular interconnected-node symbol
- **Wordmark:** mark plus uppercase `ARVYA`

### Preferred use

- Use the full wordmark for covers, profiles, banners, end cards, and first brand exposure.
- Use the mark alone only when Arvya is already identified or space is constrained.
- On dark charcoal, use the white logo.
- On white or off-white, use the charcoal logo.
- Use gold as a field or accent around the logo; do not recolor the full logo gold by default.

### Clear space and minimum size

- Clear space: at least the width of one logo mark on every side.
- Digital wordmark minimum: 120 px wide.
- Digital mark minimum: 24 px.
- Print wordmark minimum: 32 mm wide.
- Print mark minimum: 8 mm.

### Do not

- Stretch, skew, rotate, outline, bevel, or add a glow.
- Place the logo over a busy image without a solid contrast field.
- Put the logo inside an arbitrary badge.
- Change letter spacing or recreate the wordmark with typed text.
- Combine the white logo with a light background or the charcoal logo with a dark background.

## 3. Core color palette

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---:|---|
| Charcoal 990 | `#151A23` | Primary dark canvas, premium institutional field |
| Arvya Gold 60 | `#CF9742` | Primary accent, active controls, key highlights |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Primary light canvas and text on charcoal |
| Mist 200 | `#F4F4F5` | Soft light canvas, cards, alternate sections |

### Recommended proportions

- Charcoal: 45–60%
- White and light neutrals: 30–45%
- Gold: 5–10%
- Semantic colors: under 5%, only when they carry meaning

Gold is an accent, not a full-page default. Large gold fields weaken the restrained institutional tone.

## 5. Accessibility color rules

- White on Charcoal is the default high-contrast pair: **17.44:1**.
- Gold on Charcoal is accessible for normal text: **6.78:1**.
- Charcoal on Gold is accessible for normal text: **6.78:1**.
- White on Brand Gold is **2.57:1** and fails WCAG AA for text.
- For gold buttons, use `#151A23` text. Do not use white text on `#CF9742`.
- For text on white, use `#151A23`, `#252A31`, `#3A424E`, or `#4F565F` according to hierarchy.
- Do not use Gray/500 or lighter for small text on white.
- Never rely on color alone to communicate status.

## 6. Typography

### Primary display typeface — Brawler

Use **Brawler Regular (400)** for:

- Campaign headlines
- Presentation and report titles
- Large pull quotes
- Major section headings
- High-value proof statements

Brawler provides the editorial, established-finance character. Do not use artificial bold, condensed, or all-caps Brawler.

### Primary sans-serif — Instrument Sans

Use **Instrument Sans Regular (400)** for:

- Body copy
- Captions and annotations
- Supporting copy
- Data labels
- Long-form marketing text

Use **Instrument Sans SemiBold (600)** for:

- Buttons and calls to action
- Eyebrows and navigation
- Short labels
- Metadata and emphasis

### Deprecated typeface

**Mulish appears in one legacy Figma contact module. Do not use Mulish in new marketing.** Convert future work to Instrument Sans.

## 7. Layout and composition

- Use generous whitespace and one dominant message per frame.
- Keep copy blocks narrow: roughly 45–70 characters per line.
- Favor asymmetric editorial layouts over dense dashboard grids.
- Use a 12-column desktop grid and an 8 px spacing base.
- Standard outer margins: 80 px desktop, 24 px mobile.
- Standard card radius: 4 px for controls; 12–24 px for editorial containers.
- Use thin `#E6E7EA` or translucent-white rules to structure information.
- Use gold to direct attention to one action, phrase, or proof point per composition.

## 8. Visual language

### Preferred motifs

- Source citations, reference markers, highlighted evidence, and document fragments
- Deal-memory or knowledge-graph relationships
- Outlook and Microsoft 365 context shown as real product proof
- Fine lines, nodes, and restrained interconnected patterns derived from the logo
- Architectural geometry and quiet institutional environments

### Photography

Use:

- Documentary-feeling financial work environments
- Real product-in-context imagery
- Architectural details with clean geometry
- Natural, controlled light and restrained color
- Diverse professionals shown working, reviewing, or deciding

Avoid:

- Neon AI brains, robots, holograms, and blue-purple “AI” gradients
- Handshakes, staged boardroom smiles, and generic city skylines
- Crypto aesthetics or exaggerated futurism
- Images without a clear relationship to deal work or evidence

### Product screenshots

- Show one workflow at a time.
- Crop tightly around the proof.
- Preserve real UI proportions.
- Add one concise annotation or citation cue.
- Never fabricate customer data or imply an integration that is not available.

## 9. Voice and tone

### Voice

- **Precise:** make specific claims and name the workflow.
- **Credible:** lead with evidence, security, and control.
- **Concise:** short sentences; remove empty AI language.
- **Calm:** no hype, fear, or exaggerated transformation claims.
- **Deal-literate:** use the language of bankers and investors accurately.

### Preferred vocabulary

Source-backed, source-cited, deal memory, deal context, Outlook-native, approval-first, traceable, auditable, persistent, enterprise-ready.

### Avoid

Revolutionary, game-changing, magical, autonomous employee, replace your team, one-click everything, hallucination-free, guaranteed, fully autonomous.

### Copy pattern

1. State the operational problem.
2. Name the Arvya behavior.
3. Show the source, approval, or control mechanism.
4. End with a concrete outcome.

Example:

> Your team rotates. The context doesn’t. Arvya maps every email, transcript, tracker, and document into a source-cited deal memory, so the next analyst starts with the full history.

## 10. Marketing templates

### LinkedIn banner

- Charcoal field
- Full white wordmark in the upper-left safe area
- One Brawler headline
- One short Instrument Sans support line
- Gold accent limited to a rule, citation marker, or one highlighted phrase

### Social post

- 16:9 preferred for Arvya social feed content
- One idea per card
- Maximum 12–18 words in the headline
- Use a source/citation proof element whenever the claim allows it
- End carousel with a simple CTA and full wordmark

### Presentation cover

- 60–70% negative space
- Brawler title in white or charcoal
- Instrument Sans metadata
- Gold accent under 10% of the composition

### Case-study cover

- Lead with the customer outcome, not the feature name
- Include the customer logo only with approval
- Use real metrics only when verified and attributable

### Event or webinar graphic

- Prioritize title, date, and speaker hierarchy
- Use speaker photography consistently cropped
- Avoid decorative icon clutter

## 11. Domain and naming governance

- Write the brand name as **Arvya** in prose and **ARVYA** only when reproducing the wordmark.
- Use **Arvya** as the canonical public brand signature in generated social creative.
- Do not use **Arvya.io** in generated social posts.
- Product and integration names retain their official capitalization: Outlook, Microsoft 365, Excel, OneDrive, Azure.

## 12. Preflight checklist

- Correct logo variant and clear space
- Brawler only for display; Instrument Sans everywhere else
- No Mulish in new work
- Core palette values match this guide
- Gold stays an accent
- Gold buttons use charcoal text
- One dominant message per frame
- Claims are specific and supportable
- Product screenshots use real or clearly labeled demo data
- Security language has approval
- Public domain is confirmed
- Social safe zones and mobile legibility are checked
